Lab 4 - Pseudocoding & Problem-solving

Challenge:

For this lab, we had to figure out how to break complicated tasks into small steps that are easy to understand.

Problems:

The only problem I had while doing this lab was trying to come up with tasks that were interesting and complicated enough to break into several small steps.

Results:

Putting griptape on a skateboard:

  1. Get a skateboard deck, a sheet of griptape, a knife, and a file.
  2. Start peeling the paper sheet off the back of the griptape.
  3. Line the griptape up so it covers the entire deck.
  4. Press the exposed part of the griptape down onto the deck.
  5. Push the air bubbles in the grip out towards the uncovered part of the deck.
  6. Keep peeling the paper back off the grip, little by little.
  7. Repeat steps 4-6 until the deck is covered.
  8. File the edges of the grip down until there is a clear outline of the deck.
  9. Cut the excess griptape off the edges of the deck.
  10. Use the pieces of grip you cut off to sand down the edges of the griptape.
